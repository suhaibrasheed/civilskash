Thailand Parliament Dissolved even before starting.

Thailand’s PM Anutin Charnvirakul dissolved the House of Representatives just three months after taking office. The royal decree mandates elections within 45–60 days , placing polling in late Jan–early Feb 2026.
Anutin, leading the Bhumjaithai Party, headed a minority coalition after replacing a PM removed by the Constitutional Court.
Escalating tensions along the Thailand–Cambodia border and internal coalition fractures pushed the early dissolution.
Thailand has witnessed three prime ministers in two years, highlighting persistent political instability.
